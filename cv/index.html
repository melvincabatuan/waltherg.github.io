Professional Experience

PhD Researcher (2010 - present)

John Innes Centre, Norwich Research Park, Norwich, United Kingdom

Research in computational biology: Cell-Cell Signalling and Tissue Polarity in Plant Morphogenesis.

I implemented a graph-theoretic framework that facilitates bifurcation analysis of large reaction networks. I solved a range of combinatorial problems by rephrasing them as graph problems and solving them with classical graph algorithms. Enumeration of all cliques of a graph was not available through NetworkX and I implemented an algorithm based on a published article.

I also implemented variants of Crank-Nicolson to integrate numerically a convection-diffusion-reaction system. I used timing techniques, NumPy, and inline C to identify bottlenecks in my implementation and to optimize my code for speed.

For this project I implemented Gillespie's stochastic simulation algorithm to simulate a reaction-diffusion system on a one-dimensional domain. Since my initial MATLAB implementation was too slow I rewrote time critical code in C.
